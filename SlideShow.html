Study Area

The study area for this project is focused on Maryland. According to the Governor’s Office of Crime Control and Prevention, Maryland had 185,422 crimes in 2013 alone. This included 27,720 violent crimes and 157,702 property crimes. Importance

Often times, the general public do not recognize the reality of student debt and crime rates and their impact on the environment and the public population. Crime rates have a major influence on the viability of a location for the ideal home. Creating a GIS application for this project will allow users to have an interactive map that allows them to view data for student debt, crime, and median single family home rental costs. This is essential information every potential homeowner needs. An individual needs to consider not only the viability of the area of choice based on accessibility to transportation and schools, but also crime statistics and the wealth of the neighborhood, as these statistics greatly influence the population.

Objective

The objective of this project is to aid new home renters and buyers to make an informative decision when finding a place to live. The way this task is completed is by incorporating data for crime statistics, air pollution and housing costs and analyzing the best fit for the user. Often times, new home renters and buyers only make decisions on housing by researching housing costs and travel distance to their workplace. Though, this is an essential contributor to choosing a home, the livelihood of a person is far more valuable. So, what better way of insuring this than finding out what are the appropriate living conditions that are best fitting to the buyer/renter’s requirements.
